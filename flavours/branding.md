# Flavour: branded versions

A "flavour" is a skin over this corpus — an EB Labs help page, a partner site, a marketing hand-out built by the agency (Bopper), a printed FAQ. The deal that makes sharing possible:

## A flavour MAY

- Restyle everything: layout, typography, colors, logo, illustrations.
- Select a subset of questions relevant to its audience.
- Reorder and regroup questions.
- Translate, provided the translation is reviewed against the charter.
- Add its own surrounding content (intro, contact info, CTAs to its own services) — clearly outside the Q&A blocks.

## A flavour MAY NOT

- Alter the substance of any answer, including "small" softenings or sharpenings.
- Remove source citations or "What we don't know yet" notes from answers it includes.
- Add price framing, blame framing, or advocacy adjectives inside Q&A content.
- Present the content as exclusive ("our research") — attribution follows the license (proposed CC BY 4.0).
- Cherry-pick to mislead: if a flavour includes an incident question, it includes the whole answer.

## Change flow

Flavours never edit content locally. A flavour that needs a changed or new answer proposes it upstream here; once merged, every flavour benefits. This is the entire point of the single-source model — one vetted truth, many faces.

## Attribution line (proposed)

> FAQ content from the BSV Plain Answers project, used under CC BY 4.0.
