**Short answer.** The Association does not run exchanges or decide what they list. It does not hold anyone's coins and cannot get lost coins back. It does not control the network and cannot undo a payment. It does not set fees or prices. And it does not hand out licenses — government regulators do that. When something goes wrong somewhere in the BSV world, people often blame the Association anyway — this page lists, plainly, what is and isn't in its hands.

## More detail

The concrete list:

- **Exchanges.** The Association does not own or run any exchange, does not decide which exchange offers BSV ([[what-is-a-listing]], [[why-do-exchanges-delist]]), and has no say in whether an exchange gets its license — that is between the exchange and its regulator ([[orange-gateway-mica]], [[why-do-licenses-matter]]).
- **Your coins.** It cannot see or touch anyone's wallet or exchange account, cannot freeze or recover money ([[lost-keys-recovery]]), and will never ask for your keys. Anyone asking for your keys in its name is a scammer ([[keeping-bsv-safe]]).
- **The network.** Payments are processed by independent businesses ([[who-runs-the-bsv-network]]). The Association maintains the software those businesses may choose to use — that is influence, not control ([[bsva-controls-bsv]]). It cannot undo a payment or block anyone's address.
- **Markets.** It does not set the fees ([[what-are-fees]]), does not trade, and does not comment on price — and neither does this FAQ ([[no-price-talk]]).
- **Licenses.** It is not a regulator. It cannot give any company a license, and it cannot take one away ([[who-regulates-crypto]]).

What it *does* do is look after the technology itself — the software, the standards, education, and spreading the word ([[what-is-the-bsv-association]]). That is real work, but it is a completely different job from the powers people often imagine it has.
