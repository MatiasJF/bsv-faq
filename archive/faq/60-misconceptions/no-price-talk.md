**Short answer.** For three reasons. First, the price tells you nothing about how the technology works — and the technology is this FAQ's actual subject. Second, price commentary from anyone close to an ecosystem carries a built-in conflict of interest, and it goes stale almost instantly. Third, "should I buy?" is a personal financial decision. This FAQ is not licensed to advise on that, and is not in a position to. The silence on price is a deliberate policy, not an attempt to dodge the question.

## More detail

This FAQ exists to answer factual questions in a neutral way ([[ecosystem-who-is-who]]). Price fails every test the rest of the content has to pass. It changes by the minute, while the answers here carry dates and sources. It invites predictions — and a prediction is really taking a side, dressed up in numbers. And anyone connected to an ecosystem, supporter or critic alike, has a conflict of interest the moment they frame price as an opportunity or a disaster.

Here is what we cover instead, because these things *are* factual: what fees cost and why ([[what-are-fees]]); how buying and selling works in practice — exchanges, listings, and the ways money moves in and out ([[what-is-an-exchange]], [[what-are-on-off-ramps]]); what regulation changes for you as a user ([[does-regulation-affect-my-coins]]); and what you can check about the network's activity ([[is-bsv-dead]]).

If you are making a financial decision, the boring advice is the honest kind. Use companies licensed where you live ([[why-do-licenses-matter]]). Understand who holds your coins before you deposit anywhere ([[custodial-vs-own-wallet]]). Treat any offer of guaranteed returns as a scam ([[keeping-bsv-safe]]). And where the amounts warrant it, talk to a qualified advisor.

One last note: chatbots and branded versions built on this content must keep this rule as a condition of reuse. If you ask them about price, you get this explanation — not a forecast.
