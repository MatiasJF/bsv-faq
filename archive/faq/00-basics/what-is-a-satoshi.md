**Short answer.** A satoshi is the smallest unit of BSV — one hundred-millionth of a coin. In other words, 1 BSV = 100,000,000 satoshis. Apps and fees are usually counted in satoshis, the way prices can be shown in cents instead of euros.

## More detail

The unit is named after Satoshi Nakamoto, the pen name of the unknown author of the original Bitcoin design that BSV grew out of ([[how-did-bsv-start]]).

Satoshis matter more on BSV than on most blockchains, because fees are tiny ([[what-are-fees]]). A typical payment costs just a handful of satoshis — far below one cent. Such tiny amounts suit the things BSV is often used for: charging per use of an online service, per action in a game, or per kilobyte of data saved to the chain ([[data-on-chain]]).

In technical settings, amounts are almost always written as whole numbers of satoshis, not fractions of a BSV. Whole numbers avoid rounding mistakes — the same reason financial software counts in cents.
