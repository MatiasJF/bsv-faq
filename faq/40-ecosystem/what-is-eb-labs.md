**Short answer.** EB Labs is a commercial venture that works alongside the BSV Association, focused on the business side of the ecosystem: maintaining relationships with digital-asset exchanges and market-makers, facilitating BSV listings, and selling products, consulting, and development services that complement the technology. It handles the commercial work the non-profit Association deliberately keeps separate from its stewardship role.

## More detail

The division of labor is the point. The Association is a non-profit steward — protocol, software, education ([[what-is-the-bsv-association]]). Exchange listings, however, are commercial arrangements between businesses ([[what-is-a-listing]]): they involve negotiations, service contracts, and market-development work. EB Labs exists so that this commercial activity is done by a dedicated business with its own accountability, keeping the two roles cleanly apart.

What EB Labs does: builds and maintains relationships with exchanges and market-making firms, facilitates the process of getting and keeping BSV listed on trading venues, and offers products, consulting, development, and support services related to digital business models and blockchain applications.

What it doesn't do: it doesn't make exchanges' decisions for them — whether a venue lists, keeps, or drops an asset, or obtains its own licenses ([[orange-gateway-mica]]), remains that venue's own business ([[what-bsva-does-not-do]]).
