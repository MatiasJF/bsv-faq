**Short answer.** No. The Association looks after BSV, but it does not control it — and the difference is one you can check. The Association writes and maintains the main software and the technical standards. But the network is run by independent companies, and each one chooses for itself whether to use any new version. Making software that others choose to run is influence through usefulness, not control through authority.

## More detail

It is easy to see why people think this. The Association is the most visible organization around BSV. It maintains the main software ([[how-is-bsv-developed]]), and it speaks publicly for the technology. Being visible can look like being in charge. A fair test is to ask what real control would allow:

- Could it undo a payment, freeze someone's coins, or block an address? No. Nobody can do that — the system was built that way ([[how-do-transactions-work]], [[what-bsva-does-not-do]]).
- Could it force the companies running the network to accept a software change? No. Those companies are independent businesses that use what works for them ([[who-runs-the-bsv-network]]). If they reject a new version, it simply doesn't run. Blockchain history shows what happens when these companies disagree — the network splits. BSV itself began that way ([[how-did-bsv-start]]).
- Could it set the fees, or decide where BSV is traded? No. Fees come out of the open market ([[what-are-fees]]). Trading venues make their own decisions about what they list ([[what-is-a-listing]]).

What the Association *can* do is real, but limited. It can guide the direction of the software, look after the standards, fund education, and encourage people to use BSV ([[what-is-the-bsv-association]]). Even its say over the software is narrowed by BSV's "set in stone" policy: a stated commitment to keep the basic rules stable rather than steer them.

Why does the difference matter? If people believe the Association controls BSV, they end up blaming it for things it cannot decide ([[orange-gateway-mica]], [[exchange-problems-mean-bsv-problems]]). They also miss who actually made a given decision ([[ecosystem-who-is-who]]).
