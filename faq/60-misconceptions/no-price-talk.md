**Short answer.** Three reasons: price says nothing about how the technology works (this FAQ's actual subject); price commentary from any ecosystem-adjacent source is inherently conflicted and instantly stale; and "should I buy?" is a personal financial decision this FAQ is neither licensed nor positioned to advise on. The silence is deliberate policy, not evasion.

## More detail

This repository exists to answer factual questions neutrally ([[ecosystem-who-is-who]]), and price fails every test that the rest of the content must pass. It changes by the minute while answers here carry dates and sources. It invites prediction, and predictions are advocacy in numeric clothing. And any source connected to an ecosystem — critic or supporter — has a conflict of interest the moment it frames price as opportunity or doom.

What we cover instead, because these *are* factual: what fees cost and why ([[what-are-fees]]), how market access works — exchanges, listings, ramps ([[what-is-an-exchange]], [[what-are-on-off-ramps]]) — what regulation changes for users ([[does-regulation-affect-my-coins]]), and what's verifiable about network activity ([[is-bsv-dead]]).

If you're making a financial decision, the boring advice is the honest kind: use licensed venues for your jurisdiction ([[why-do-licenses-matter]]), understand custody before depositing ([[custodial-vs-own-wallet]]), treat guaranteed-return offers as scams ([[keeping-bsv-safe]]), and consult a qualified advisor where stakes warrant it.

Chatbots and branded versions built on this content inherit this rule ([[what-is-eb-labs]] and other flavours included): price questions get this rationale, not a forecast.
