**Short answer.** Orange Gateway, an independent Icelandic exchange known for its BSV/fiat trading, announced on 30 June 2026 that it was not granted a MiCA license by the Central Bank of Iceland. It suspended deposits and trading immediately and asked customers to withdraw all funds within 60 days. If its appeal fails, the company expects to close the platform permanently.

## More detail

In their own words: Orange Gateway stated that its application "was not fully processed due to incomplete information," that it is appealing the decision, that deposits and trading are suspended with immediate effect, and that customers should withdraw all funds and digital assets within 60 days. If the appeal fails, the company expects to permanently discontinue the platform.

The timing follows the EU-wide rule change: under MiCA ([[what-is-mica]]), the transition period for crypto-asset service providers ended on 1 July 2026. From that date, any provider without a MiCA license must stop offering these services in the EU and the EEA (the European Economic Area — the EU plus Iceland, Liechtenstein, and Norway, which adopt the EU's market rules). Iceland participates through the EEA, which is why the Central Bank of Iceland decides Orange Gateway's authorization. Orange Gateway is far from alone — industry coverage around the deadline reported that a large majority of European crypto firms had not yet obtained a license, affecting millions of users across many platforms and many different cryptocurrencies.

What this means in practice: Orange Gateway customers should follow the company's withdrawal instructions within the stated window ([[exchange-closes-my-funds]]). Coins held in your own wallet ([[custodial-vs-own-wallet]]) are not part of any exchange's wind-down, and the venues serving each region are covered in [[where-to-get-bsv]].

Who decided what: the MiCA licensing decision was made by the regulator, the Central Bank of Iceland, about one company, Orange Gateway, which is responsible for its own regulatory compliance. Orange Gateway is independent — it is not owned or operated by any organization in the BSV ecosystem ([[ecosystem-who-is-who]], [[what-bsva-does-not-do]]).

## What we don't know yet

- The outcome of Orange Gateway's appeal, and whether the platform will close permanently.
- The specifics of what information the regulator considered incomplete (only Orange Gateway's own characterization is public).
