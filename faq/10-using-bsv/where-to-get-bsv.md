**Short answer.** There are three common ways. You can buy BSV on a digital-asset exchange that lists it. You can receive it directly from another person or business — BSV is a payment network, so anyone can send it to your wallet. Or you can earn it by selling goods, services, or data in apps that accept BSV. Which exchanges you can use depends on your country and its rules.

## More detail

**Exchanges** — companies where you swap regular money for coins and back — are the usual way to buy BSV. What is available depends on where you live: an exchange must be licensed for the place you live in, so the list differs between, say, the EU, the UK, and Asia. It also changes over time, as companies gain — or fail to gain — the licenses their markets require. Before you deposit money, check the exchange's approval status for your country. And remember: coins left on an exchange are held by that company, not by you ([[custodial-vs-own-wallet]]).

**Receiving directly** needs no company in the middle at all. Install a wallet ([[what-is-a-wallet]]), share your address or QR code, and anyone can pay you — across borders, in seconds, for a fraction of a cent ([[sending-bsv-basics]]).

**Earning** is increasingly common in BSV apps — small earnings for content, gaming, or data — because tiny payments are practical on this blockchain ([[what-are-fees]]).

In line with this FAQ's rules, we do not recommend specific companies or discuss whether you should buy at all. This answer explains *how* access works — not *whether* or *where* you should buy.
