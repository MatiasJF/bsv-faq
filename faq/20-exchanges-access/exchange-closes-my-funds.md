**Short answer.** Follow the exchange's official instructions, and act promptly. Closures normally come with a set period for taking your coins out — Orange Gateway's 2026 notice gave customers 60 days ([[orange-gateway-mica]]). Move your coins to a wallet that only you control — like taking your money out of a locker and into your own pocket. Check the address and the network carefully before you send. And be very suspicious of anyone who contacts *you* about the closure — closures attract scammers.

## More detail

A practical order of steps:

1. **Check the news at the source.** Go to the exchange's own website or its verified account — not screenshots or messages sent to you. Scammers use closure panic to set up fake "fast withdrawal" websites ([[keeping-bsv-safe]]).
2. **Get your own wallet first,** if you do not have one. Install a reputable wallet app and keep a copy of its recovery phrase somewhere safe, offline ([[what-is-a-wallet]], [[custodial-vs-own-wallet]]).
3. **Take your coins out within the window.** Check the address and the network before you confirm ([[sending-bsv-basics]]). Send a small test amount first. If you miss the window, your claim usually moves into a slower legal wind-down process.
4. **Save your records** — your transaction history and statements — while you can still log in. You may need them for taxes or claims.

Why do closures happen? Often it is licensing. An exchange that is not granted a required authorization must stop serving that market ([[why-do-licenses-matter]], [[what-is-mica]]). An announced wind-down with a withdrawal window is an orderly process, with a clear path for customers. A sudden insolvency — a company collapse — is not. For anyone with money there, that is the practical difference between the two.

One thing worth repeating: when your coins sit on an exchange, that company holds them for you — which is exactly why this page exists. Coins already in your own wallet are untouched by anything that happens to any exchange ([[exchange-problems-mean-bsv-problems]]).
