**Short answer.** A wallet is an app that holds the keys to your coins and lets you send and receive them. The coins themselves live on the blockchain — the shared public record. The wallet holds your private keys: the secret codes that prove the coins are yours and let you spend them.

## More detail

The name is slightly misleading. A wallet does not hold coins the way a leather wallet holds cash. Your coins are entries in the public record. What the wallet app keeps safe is your keys — the secret codes that let you, and only you, move those entries.

Wallets come in two very different kinds, and the difference matters more than any list of features ([[custodial-vs-own-wallet]]):

- **Self-custody wallets.** You hold the keys, on your own device. Nobody can freeze or move your coins — and nobody can help you if you lose the keys ([[lost-keys-recovery]]).
- **Custodial services.** An exchange or app holds the keys for you, the way a bank holds your deposits. That is convenient. But your access depends on that company staying in business, properly licensed, and online ([[exchange-closes-my-funds]]).

Two practical basics: keep your recovery phrase written down, offline, and private ([[keeping-bsv-safe]]). And treat any request to "verify" that phrase as a scam — no honest service ever asks for it.
